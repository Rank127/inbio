## IRR Analysis — $3M Convertible Note

### Scenario 1: Convert at Series A (Year 2, moderate valuation)

**Assumptions:** iNBIO raises a $15M Series A at $30M pre-money valuation to fund the 75 TPD unit. The $3M convertible note converts at a 20% discount ($24M effective valuation).

| Year | Cash Flow |
|------|-----------|
| 0 (2026) | -$3,000,000 (investment) |
| 1 | $210,000 (7% interest) |
| 2 | $3,750,000 (principal converts to equity worth $3M / 0.80 = $3.75M at 20% discount) |

**IRR: ~16.4%**
**MoM: 1.32x** (total return of $3,960,000 on $3,000,000 invested over 2 years)

### Scenario 2: Convert at Series A (Year 2, strong valuation)

**Assumptions:** Demo plant proves out with strong revenue. Series A at $50M pre-money. Convertible note converts at $40M effective valuation.

| Year | Cash Flow |
|------|-----------|
| 0 | -$3,000,000 |
| 1 | $210,000 |
| 2 | $3,750,000 (equity value at conversion) + $210,000 interest |

**IRR: ~16.4%** (same — IRR on a convertible is driven by the discount, not the valuation, until there's a liquidity event)

### Scenario 3: Hold equity post-conversion to 300 TPD revenue (Year 5)

**Assumptions:** Convert at Year 2, hold equity. 300 TPD plant generates $46M EBITDA by 2030. At 6x EBITDA multiple = $276M enterprise value. $3M at 20% discount to a $30M round = 12.5% ownership stake at conversion. Assuming 30% dilution in subsequent rounds = ~8.75% stake.

| Year | Cash Flow |
|------|-----------|
| 0 | -$3,000,000 |
| 1 | $210,000 |
| 2 | $210,000 (interest — note converts to equity) |
| 5 | $24,150,000 (8.75% of $276M enterprise value) |

**IRR: ~55%**
**MoM: 8.2x**

### Scenario 4: Downside — Demo plant underperforms, note repaid at maturity

| Year | Cash Flow |
|------|-----------|
| 0 | -$3,000,000 |
| 2 | $3,420,000 (principal + 2 years interest at 7%) |

**IRR: 7.0%**
**MoM: 1.14x**

## Key Risk Factors

1. **Construction risk** — 6 TPD demo completion depends on this raise closing
2. **Technology risk** — Pilot must demonstrate target yields at sustained commercial operation
3. **Scale-up risk** — Moving from 6 TPD to 75 TPD involves engineering challenges
4. **Market risk** — Bio-oil and biochar prices may fluctuate; carbon credit pricing is volatile
5. **Capital risk** — 75 TPD and 300 TPD phases require significant additional capital ($55M total)
6. **Regulatory risk** — 45Z tax credits, EQIP funding, and carbon credit methodologies subject to policy changes
7. **Liquidity risk** — Equity in a private company is illiquid; no guaranteed exit timeline

## What This Round Unlocks

The $3M does not fund the full commercial scale-up. It funds the **proof point** that makes everything after it possible:

1. **Eliminates the $1.4M bank debt** — clean balance sheet for future fundraising
2. **Completes the 6 TPD demo** — the first operating plant producing real product
3. **Generates first revenue** — bio-oil, biochar, and carbon credit sales from a working plant
4. **Creates the data package** for the next round — real yield data, real operating costs, real customer feedback
5. **Positions for Series A** — with a running demo and ENERGYWERX-designed 75 TPD reactor, iNBIO can raise $15-25M for commercial scale
