## Memo
*(Replace the current paragraph. This is your pitch — the full story investors read on your page. Paste the following into the rich text editor:)*

---

**The Problem**

A billion tons of agricultural and forestry waste piles up in the U.S. every year. Sawmills pay to haul it away. Farmers burn it in the open. Municipalities landfill it. All of it releases CO2 and methane — while industries pay premium prices for heating oil, soil amendments, and carbon offsets that this waste could produce.

The technology to convert waste into valuable products has existed for decades. What didn't exist was a modular, affordable system a sawmill or municipality could actually deploy without a $50 million construction project.

**Our Solution**

We built one. iNBIO's fast pyrolysis reactor heats biomass to 450-550°C in the absence of oxygen. In under two seconds, it breaks apart into three products:

- **Bio-oil (40-70% of input)** — A dense liquid fuel that directly replaces fossil heating oil. At its highest value, bio-oil is the feedstock for sustainable aviation fuel.
- **Biochar (15-40% of input)** — A stable, carbon-rich solid used in agriculture, stormwater filtration, environmental remediation, and concrete. Every ton locks away 700-800 kg of CO2 for centuries, qualifying for carbon removal credits worth $100-250 per ton.
- **Syngas (7-20% of input)** — A combustible gas we recycle back to heat the reactor. No external fuel needed. The process runs itself.

Our systems are modular: factory-built in standard 40-foot shipping containers, trucked to site, and interconnected. A 6-ton-per-day demonstration plant is currently under construction — this round funds its completion. A 75-ton-per-day commercial reactor has already been designed through an ENERGYWERX grant, positioning us for rapid scale-up once the demonstration plant is operational.

**How We Make Money**

Three revenue streams from one integrated process:

1. **Product sales** — We sell engineered biochar and bio-oil to agriculture, stormwater, construction, and industrial energy customers. The biochar market is approaching $1 billion and 62% of high-quality supply is already locked up by repeat buyers.

2. **Build + Operate** — We design, build, and operate pyrolysis plants at client sites. Three engagement models: full-service (we run it), turnkey (we build it, you run it), or build-train-transfer. Clients include sawmills, municipalities, and agricultural operations.

3. **OEM Equipment** — We sell modular pyrolysis systems to operators who want to own and run their own plant. Systems range from 5 to 75 tons per day, with installation support and operator training.

On top of all three, every ton of biochar generates carbon removal credits at $100-250 per ton CO2. A 25-ton-per-day system could generate $200,000-$400,000 annually in credit revenue alone — stacked on top of product sales.

**Why Now**

The market conditions have never been stronger:

- The biochar market hit $970 million in 2026, growing 13% annually
- ICVCM approved three biochar credit methodologies — institutional credibility is here
- Carbon credits average $164 per ton, up 25% in two years. Microsoft, JP Morgan, and Shopify are buying
- Holcim just poured net-zero concrete using biochar at Canary Wharf — a new application category is emerging
- 62% of high-quality biochar capacity is locked into contracts. Supply cannot keep up with demand

**The Team**

Led by Raj Kathuria, who designed the reactor, characterized the feedstocks, and certified the products. Decades of experience in biotechnology, process engineering, and cleantech commercialization. This isn't a team that hired engineers — this is the engineering team.

**Use of Funds**

This round funds the next phase of scaling:

- 40% — Equipment and manufacturing: fabricate and deploy next production system
- 20% — Facility and site development: site prep, utilities, feedstock handling
- 15% — Feedstock supply chain: agreements with forestry and agricultural operations
- 15% — Sales and marketing: commercial team, industry events, OEM pipeline
- 10% — Working capital: operators, production runs, certifications, carbon credit registration

**What Happens Next**

- Q2 2026 — Close round. Finalize equipment procurement

- Q4 2026 — System delivery and commissioning. First production runs
- Q1 2027 — Steady-state production. Product shipments. First carbon credits issued
