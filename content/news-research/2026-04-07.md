## ICVCM Just Gave Biochar Credits Its Biggest Credibility Boost Yet

The Integrity Council for the Voluntary Carbon Market approved three biochar methodologies in one shot: CAR's U.S. and Canada Biochar (V1.0), Isometric's Biochar Production and Storage (V1.0), and Verra's VM0044 (V1.2). This is the stamp of legitimacy that institutional buyers have been waiting for. Between Isometric's 25 registered projects (~500k credits in 2026) and VM0044's 3 projects (~249k credits annually), we're looking at roughly 750,000 biochar credits hitting the market this year. And 40% are already pre-sold. ICVCM Chair Annette Nazareth called biochar "a rapidly growing segment of the carbon market" — which is corporate-speak for "this is real now."

**Why it matters for iNBIO:** Every one of our biochar customers can now point to ICVCM-approved methodologies when pitching carbon credit revenue to their boards. This makes our /carbon-credits page more relevant than ever — and strengthens the revenue diversification story for the WeFunder raise.

Sources: [Carbon Herald](https://carbonherald.com/icvcm-approves-new-biochar-and-ifm-methodologies/), [Sylvera](https://www.sylvera.com/blog/biochar-carbon-credits)

## Biochar Credit Prices Hit $164/ton — Up 25% in Two Years

Average biochar credit pricing reached $164/tonne in 2025, up from $131 in 2023. Microsoft bought 129k tonnes in Q1 2024 alone. JP Morgan Chase is in the market. Biochar Carbon Removal accounted for 89.4% of all durable CDR deliveries in Q2 2025 — nothing else is even close. The market is projected to hit $1.35 billion by 2030. But here's the nuance Sylvera flagged: buyers need to watch for poor lifecycle assessments that ignore transport emissions, questionable feedstock classification, and lack of end-use monitoring. The gap between high-quality and low-quality biochar credits is widening, and pricing will follow.

**Why it matters for iNBIO:** Our controlled pyrolysis process with characterized feedstocks positions us on the quality side of this split. A 500-ton/year biochar operation at $164/ton is $82k in credit revenue alone — and prices are trending up, not down.

Sources: [Sylvera](https://www.sylvera.com/blog/biochar-carbon-credits), [Regreener](https://www.regreener.earth/blog/the-5-best-biochar-carbon-credit-and-offset-projects-of-2026)

## Holcim Pours Net-Zero Concrete at Canary Wharf Using Biochar from Coffee Grounds

Holcim UK and Canary Wharf Group achieved concrete with a projected -14 kgCO2e/m3 footprint. They made biochar from coffee grounds collected from Canary Wharf retailers and coppiced wood, then embedded it in the concrete mix. First pour was April 2025 on a test slab, followed by two-metre-deep raft slabs in September 2025. The initial mix hit 69 kgCO2e/m3 — an 80% reduction — and further optimization pushed it negative. They literally turned a building foundation into a carbon sink.

This isn't a lab demo. It's full-scale construction pours on a real London development. And Holcim is listing biochar concrete as one of their "5 construction innovations to watch in 2026."

**Why it matters for iNBIO:** Our biochar-in-concrete application (/biochar-applications#concrete) is the most underappreciated part of our story. Holcim proving it works at scale in one of the world's most prominent developments validates the entire category. If developers start specifying biochar concrete, demand for consistent, engineered biochar explodes.

Sources: [Holcim](https://www.holcim.co.uk/news-and-resources/press-releases/Net-Zero-Concrete-Achievement), [ESG News](https://esgnews.com/holcim-uk-delivers-net-zero-concrete-using-carbon-storing-biochar/), [World Cement](https://www.worldcement.com/europe-cis/12032026/holcim-and-canary-wharf-group-produce-carbon-storing-concrete/)

## Biochar Market Approaching $1B — 62% of Capacity Already Locked Up

The global biochar market hit roughly $860M in 2025 and is projected at $970M for 2026, growing at ~13% CAGR toward $2.7-3.5B by mid-2030s. Agriculture still drives 55% of demand, environmental remediation about 18%. But the real signal is on the supply side: 62% of high-quality biochar capacity for 2025 was locked into contracts by repeat buyers, with another 28% secured through 2026. That's a supply-constrained market. If you can produce consistent, certified biochar, you can sell it.

The EU's Carbon Removal Certification Framework (CRCF) goes operational in 2026 — the world's first standardized voluntary certification for carbon removals. This will accelerate European demand even further.

**Why it matters for iNBIO:** Supply constraints = pricing power. This is the core argument for why building more pyrolysis capacity now (our /build-operate-plant and /oem-equipment offerings) makes sense. The market is buying faster than producers can scale.

Sources: [Future Markets](https://www.futuremarketsinc.com/the-global-biochar-market-2026-2036/), [Precedence Research](https://www.precedenceresearch.com/biochar-market), [Inkwood Research](https://www.inkwoodresearch.com/reports/north-america-biochar-market-size/)

## USDA Biochar Funding: EQIP Practice 336 Is Real Money

NRCS pays for biochar application under Conservation Practice Standard 336. Payment rates vary by state — Delaware is at $201.87 per cubic yard for biochar, and the Delta region estimates about $1,700 per acre for full biochar application (including tillage, hauling, spreading, and the material itself). The Inflation Reduction Act track specifically includes biochar as a soil carbon amendment. USDA invested $120M in six biochar initiatives across California, Arkansas, and South Dakota in late 2024.

EQIP won't fund whole-farm adoption, but it covers test acreages — which is exactly how farmers evaluate before committing. Applications accepted year-round with state-specific ranking deadlines.

**Why it matters for iNBIO:** Every farmer who applies for EQIP biochar funding is a potential biochar customer. Our /buy-biochar-biofuel page should reference these programs explicitly. The $120M USDA investment is a strong data point for the WeFunder narrative.

Sources: [US Biochar Initiative](https://biochar-us.org/usda-nrcs-biochar-funding-quick-guide), [NRCS Pennsylvania](https://www.nrcs.usda.gov/state-offices/pennsylvania/news/nrcs-announces-conservation-funding-opportunities-for-fy-2026), [NRCS EQIP](https://www.nrcs.usda.gov/programs-initiatives/environmental-quality-incentives-program)

## Minnesota Building an 8,600-ton/year Biochar Facility — and Updating Stormwater Rules

Scott County, MN approved a facility that will produce 8,600 tons of biochar per year. Meanwhile, MPCA is updating its stormwater manual with new biochar content and planning webinars for 2026. One existing Minnesota biochar-sand stormwater basin is estimated to remove 22,000 lbs of sediment, 202 lbs of nitrogen, and 50 lbs of phosphorus annually. These are the kind of hard numbers that get stormwater engineers to specify biochar.

**Why it matters for iNBIO:** Minnesota is becoming the de facto proving ground for biochar stormwater. Their data directly validates our stormwater filtration claims. Worth referencing in our next stormwater-focused content.

Sources: [MPCA Stormwater Manual](https://stormwater.pca.state.mn.us/?title=Biochar_and_applications_of_biochar_in_stormwater_management), [MPCA Biochar Production](https://www.pca.state.mn.us/business-with-us/biochar-production)

## SAF: Still Mostly Cooking Oil, But Biomass Pathways Are Coming

SAF production hit 1.3 billion liters in 2024 — just 0.3% of global jet fuel. HEFA (hydroprocessed fats and oils) still powers 95%+ of SAF flights. The DOE's SAF Grand Challenge targets 3 billion gallons by 2030 and 35 billion gallons by 2050. Lignocellulosic pathways (woody biomass, crop residues via Fischer-Tropsch or pyrolysis-to-jet) are the obvious next frontier, but costs remain high — $1.85 to $6/gallon depending on scale and feedstock. The bottleneck isn't chemistry, it's feedstock supply and refining infrastructure at scale.

**Why it matters for iNBIO:** Bio-oil-to-SAF is our highest-value upgrade pathway, but it's still pre-commercial for pyrolysis-derived bio-oil specifically. Worth watching, not worth betting the business on yet. The near-term money is in biochar and direct bio-oil sales.

Sources: [DOE](https://www.energy.gov/eere/bioenergy/sustainable-aviation-fuels), [Seed World](https://www.seedworld.com/us/2026/02/09/from-seed-to-sky-the-demand-for-sustainable-aviation-fuel/), [AIP Publishing](https://pubs.aip.org/aip/sci/article/2026/14/141104/3385864/Finding-the-right-mix-of-conventional-and)
