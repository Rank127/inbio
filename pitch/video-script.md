# iNBIO Investor Video — 2-Minute Script

**Target runtime:** 1:55–2:05
**Voiceover word count:** ~290 words (at ~150 wpm)
**Visual rule:** No founder portraits. Reactor, feedstock, product, and equipment B-roll only.
**Draft v1 — 2026-04-24**

---

## Structure

10 beats matching the 10 pitch slides — each ~12 seconds, ~20 words of VO.

---

## Scene-by-scene

### 0:00–0:12 — Opening / Hook

**Visual:** Slow push-in on the reactor in the warehouse. Cold light. Title card fades in: "iNBIO — Waste to Wealth."

**VO:**
> "What if one ton of agricultural waste — worth just twenty-five dollars — could become six hundred fifteen dollars of renewable fuel, biochar, and clean-fuel credits?"

---

### 0:12–0:24 — The Problem

**Visual:** Cut to wide shots of biomass piles (sawmill waste, crop residue). Then cut to a fuel terminal / rail car.

**VO:**
> "Industrial fuel buyers pay three to five dollars a gallon for low-carbon liquid fuel. Meanwhile, a billion tons of biomass waste sit unused across the United States every year."

---

### 0:24–0:36 — The Solution

**Visual:** Reactor schematic animation. Arrows: biomass in → bio-oil + biochar out. Cut to real footage of pyrolysis equipment in operation.

**VO:**
> "At iNBIO, our patented moving-bed fast pyrolysis reactor converts one ton of biomass into one hundred and three gallons of bio-oil plus four hundred pounds of biochar."

---

### 0:36–0:48 — Unit Economics

**Visual:** Clean text overlay — the numbers animating in:
- Input: $25
- Bio-oil sales: $412
- Biochar sales: $70
- 45Z federal credit: $103
- Carbon credits: $30
- **Total: $615**

**VO:**
> "Twenty-five dollars of input becomes six hundred fifteen dollars of revenue — a twenty-four-to-one ratio, before operating costs."

---

### 0:48–1:00 — Proprietary / IP

**Visual:** Close-up on patent certificate or cover sheet. Then cut to detail shots of reactor internals (moving bed, filter).

**VO:**
> "Our technology is protected by two issued patents — United States nine-one-two-one-six-four-four, and Canadian patent two-eight-three-two-five-five-zero — with a third application pending."

---

### 1:00–1:12 — Stage / Validation

**Visual:** Wide shot of the warehouse full of crated, ready-to-install equipment. Pan slowly. Date-stamp overlay: "Q4 2026 — Pilot Commissioning."

**VO:**
> "Ninety-five percent of our pilot equipment is already purchased and warehoused. Commissioning begins in the fourth quarter of 2026."

---

### 1:12–1:24 — Customers & Market

**Visual:** Cut to rail cars, a Baltimore harbor shot, and an oil-well capping image. Text cards pop up: "Signed," "In Discussion," "LOI in Hand."

**VO:**
> "We have a signed agreement with a Baltimore fuel distributor, active talks with rail wholesalers, and a confidential letter of intent from a carbon-credit buyer plugging depleted oil wells."

---

### 1:24–1:36 — First Plant

**Visual:** Map graphic — Eastern Shore, Maryland pin drops. Then dissolve to a commercial-scale process diagram: "75 TPD commercial unit."

**VO:**
> "Our pilot plant is going up on the Eastern Shore of Maryland. Engineering for our seventy-five-ton-per-day commercial unit — projected to scale to three hundred tons per day by twenty-thirty — is funded by this round."

---

### 1:36–1:48 — The Ask

**Visual:** Clean text card:
> **$3,000,000**
> Regulation D 506(c)
> Accredited investors only

Then animated allocation bar: Install → Operate → Engineer → Retire Debt.

**VO:**
> "We are raising three million dollars under Regulation D to install the pilot, fund six months of operations, engineer the commercial plant, and retire existing debt."

---

### 1:48–2:00 — Why Now / Close

**Visual:** Final montage — reactor, crates in warehouse, bio-oil pouring into a beaker, biochar cascade, Eastern Shore site photo. End on logo.

**VO:**
> "Equipment bought. Customer signed. Patents issued. Feedstock contracted. iNBIO is not selling the future — we are finishing a plant. Join accredited investors completing the last step. Learn more at [URL]."

## Production notes

**DO:**
- Use existing reactor footage from `20260414_134735_1.mp4` and `20260414_141921_1.mp4` if quality allows.
- Shoot additional B-roll of: warehoused equipment (if accessible), biomass feedstock, bio-oil product samples, biochar product samples.
- Use clean, modern sans-serif text overlays — not WordArt-style effects.
- Hire a professional voice actor or use a premium neural TTS (ElevenLabs or similar). Do not self-record.

**DO NOT:**
- Include founder headshots, family photos, or office selfies.
- Use stock footage of smokestacks, melting ice, or "climate crisis" imagery — that is exactly what Chinnababu flagged as generic.
- Make forward-looking claims without the standard 506(c) disclaimer at the end.

**End-card disclaimer (small text, 3 sec):**
> This communication is for accredited investors only. All forward-looking statements are subject to risks and uncertainties. See full offering disclosures at [URL].

---

## Audio

- **Music:** Quiet, instrumental, industrial. Avoid orchestral "inspirational" cues. Something closer to a product launch video than a charity appeal.
- **Sound design:** Faint machine hum under reactor shots. Subtle "clink" when numbers appear on screen.
- **Levels:** VO dominant. Music never louder than –18 dB relative to voice.
