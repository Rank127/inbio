## PITCH (long-form narrative section)

### The opportunity

Railroads, marine operators, and industrial boiler users pay **$3 to $5 per gallon** for low-carbon liquid fuel. At the same time, U.S. farms, sawmills, and forestry operations pay to dispose of **more than a billion tons** of biomass waste every year.

The waste stream and the fuel stream never meet. iNBIO connects them.

### What we do

Using our patented **moving-bed fast pyrolysis reactor**, iNBIO converts a ton of agricultural and forestry waste into:

- **103 gallons of bio-oil** — a renewable liquid fuel and a drop-in feedstock for carbon removal.
- **400 lbs of biochar** — a stable carbon product for soil amendment and industrial use.

**Per-ton economics (conservative assumptions):**

| Stream | Value |
|---|---|
| Bio-oil sales (103 gal × $4.00) | $412.00 |
| Biochar sales (0.20 tons × $350) | $70.00 |
| 45Z federal clean-fuel credit (103 gal × $1.00) | $103.00 |
| Biochar carbon credits (0.20 tons × $150) | $30.00 |
| **Total revenue per ton of feedstock** | **$615.00** |

At $25 per ton of feedstock and $615 in total per-ton revenue, the input-to-output ratio is roughly **24 to 1** before operating costs.

### Why now

- Our **pilot-plant equipment is already paid for** and sitting in a warehouse.
- Our **first customer agreement is already signed** — a Baltimore fuel distributor supplying municipal boilers.
- Our **feedstock supply agreement is already in place.**
- We hold a **confidential LOI** from a buyer using renewable oil to plug abandoned oil wells for carbon credit.

This raise is not funding a concept. It is funding the final step between warehoused equipment and revenue.

### Where the money goes

We are raising **$3,000,000** to:

1. Install and commission the pilot plant on the Eastern Shore, Maryland, by **Q4 2026**.
2. Fund the first six months of pilot operations.
3. Complete detailed engineering of the **75 tons-per-day commercial plant** — projected to produce ~7,900 gallons of bio-oil and ~9.4 tons of biochar per day.
4. Retire existing company debt.

## FAQ (suggested — keep short)

**What stage is the plant in?**
95% of the pilot-plant equipment has been purchased and is warehoused on the Eastern Shore. The Q4 2026 commissioning date assumes the installation work funded by this raise begins promptly after closing.

**Why are you relocating from Salisbury, MD?**
The original Salisbury site is held up by neighborhood litigation. Rather than wait, we are proceeding on the Eastern Shore, MD, where no such constraint exists.

**Is bio-oil the same as biodiesel?**
No. Bio-oil is a thermal-pyrolysis product used as a boiler fuel, as a feedstock for upgraded renewable fuels, and — increasingly — as a durable carbon-removal mechanism when injected into depleted oil wells. Biodiesel is a transesterification product of vegetable oils, unrelated to our process.

**What happens after the pilot is running?**
Detailed engineering for a 75 TPD commercial plant — funded in part by this raise — runs in parallel with pilot operations. The pilot validates performance for the commercial unit.

**Why a Reg D 506(c) round and not Reg CF?**
506(c) lets us raise from accredited investors under a faster, more predictable process. We may conduct a Reg CF round on WeFunder at a later stage; for now, this round is restricted to accredited investors.
